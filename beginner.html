Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to be flexible to start yoga?

Not at all! Yoga is about working with your body as it is today. Our beginner programs focus on gradually building flexibility, and our instructors provide modifications for all body types and abilities. Flexibility is a result of yoga practice, not a prerequisite.

What should I wear to class?

Comfortable, stretchy clothing that allows you to move freely is ideal. Many students wear yoga pants or leggings with a comfortable t-shirt or tank top. You'll practice barefoot, so no special footwear is needed.

Do I need to bring my own yoga mat?

We provide mats and all necessary props for our in-studio classes. If you enroll in our Complete Beginner's Package, you'll receive your own personal yoga mat and prop kit to use at home. For online classes, we recommend having a mat, but a carpeted area can work to start.

How often should I practice as a beginner?

We recommend attending 2-3 classes per week, with short (10-15 minute) home practices on non-class days if possible. Consistency is more important than duration when you're starting out. Our programs are structured to provide gradual progression without overwhelming beginners.

Are there health conditions that might prevent me from practicing yoga?

While yoga is generally safe for most people, it's important to consult with your healthcare provider before beginning any new exercise program, especially if you have chronic health conditions, recent injuries, or are pregnant. Our instructors can provide modifications for many conditions, but your safety is our priority.
