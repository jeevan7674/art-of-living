About Ayurvedha

Ayurveda or Ayurvedic medicine is a system of traditional medicine native to India, which uses a range of treatments, including panchakarma ('5 actions'), yoga, massage, acupuncture and herbal medicine, to encourage health and wellbeing.

Ayurveda therapies have varied and evolved over more than two millennia. Therapies include herbal medicines, special diets, meditation, yoga, massage, laxatives, enemas, and medical oils. Ayurvedic preparations are typically based on complex herbal compounds, minerals, and metal substances (perhaps under the influence of early Indian alchemy or rasashastra). Ancient ayurveda texts also taught surgical techniques, including rhinoplasty, kidney stone extractions, sutures, and the extraction of foreign objects.

Benefits of Ayurvedha

  1. Personalized treatment plans tailored to individual body types (doshas)
  2. Emphasis on natural remedies derived from herbs, minerals, and other natural sources
  3. Reduction of the risk of diseases by maintaining balance and harmony within the body - Effectiveness in managing chronic conditions
  4. Prevention of diseases by maintaining internal harmony and balance - Management of chronic conditions such as arthritis, digestive issues, and skin disorders
  5. Restoration of energy and vitality by aligning with natural rhythms and cycles - Support for mental clarity, emotional balance, and spiritual growth through meditation,
  6. Utilization of natural remedies derived from herbs, minerals, and other natural sources - Emphasis on holistic well-being encompassing physical, mental, and spiritual health

These are just a few of the many benefits of using ayurvedha.
